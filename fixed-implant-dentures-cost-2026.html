Fixed Implant Dentures: 2026 Price Guide & Types

Types of Fixed Implant Dentures

All-on-4

  • Uses 4 implants per arch (two straight in front, two tilted in back)
  • Usually does not require bone grafting
  • Often supports same-day temporary teeth ("Teeth in a Day")
  • Most affordable fixed option, but slightly less stable than designs with more implants

All-on-6 / All-on-X

  • Uses 5-8 implants per arch for additional stability and load distribution
  • Stronger bite force and reduced risk of implant failure compared to All-on-4
  • May require bone grafting depending on the case
  • More expensive due to additional implants and surgical complexity

Fixed Implant-Supported Bridge

  • Uses 6-8 implants per arch to support multiple connected bridge segments (e.g., "3 on 6" - 3 bridges on 6 implants)
  • No pink acrylic - preserves and uses the patient's natural gum tissue
  • Thinner, easier to clean, and feels most like natural teeth
  • Requires adequate bone height and width; not suitable for patients with significant bone loss

Hybrid Prosthesis (Fixed-Hybrid Denture)

  • A fixed full-arch prosthesis screwed onto implants (typically 4-6)
  • Includes artificial gum tissue (pink acrylic or zirconia) to replace lost bone/tissue volume
  • Available in multiple materials: acrylic on titanium frame, monolithic zirconia (up to 1200 MPa strength), PEEK, porcelain, or EMAX crowns
  • Good option when bone and gum loss make a natural-looking bridge difficult

Zygomatic Implants

  • Extra-long implants (30-52.5mm) anchored in the cheekbone rather than the jawbone
  • Designed for patients with severe upper jaw bone loss who can't get traditional implants
  • Eliminates the need for bone grafting entirely
  • Typically 2 zygomatic implants in the back + 2 conventional implants in the front
  • Survival rates of 98.4-100%

Pterygoid Implants

  • Placed in the pterygoid plates at the back of the upper jaw where bone density is higher
  • Alternative anchor point for patients with posterior upper jaw bone loss
  • Can extend the prosthesis further back for a fuller arch
  • Often combined with conventional implants in the front

Quad Zygoma

  • Uses 4 zygomatic implants (2 per cheekbone) with no conventional implants at all
  • For the most extreme cases of upper jaw bone loss where no usable jawbone remains
  • Completely graft-free solution

Transnasal Implants (Emerging)

  • Newer technique using the nasal floor area for anchorage
  • Combined with zygomatic and pterygoid implants in the most complex cases
  • Part of the emerging PATZI protocol (Pterygoid, Axial, Transnasal, Zygomatic Implants)

What's Typically Included in the Price

  • 3D imaging and consultation
  • Surgical placement of implants
  • Any necessary tooth extractions
  • Temporary prosthetics during healing
  • Final custom permanent arch
  • Follow-up care

Potential Additional Costs

  • Bone grafting: $300 - $4,000 (if jawbone density is insufficient)
  • Sinus lift: $1,500 - $3,000
  • Sedation: $500 - $1,500
  • Maintenance visits: $100 - $200 per visit (every 6 months)

Key Factors That Affect Price

  • Location: The DC/Northern Virginia metro area tends to run 20-30% higher than smaller cities
  • Material: Acrylic is the most affordable; zirconia is the most durable and natural-looking but significantly more expensive
  • Provider expertise: Board-certified specialists typically charge more
  • Case complexity: Bone loss, extractions, and additional procedures add cost
  • Number of implants: More implants means higher surgical and component costs
  • Type of implant: Zygomatic and pterygoid implants are specialized and typically cost more

Insurance

Most dental insurance plans offer limited or no coverage for implant dentures, often classifying them as elective/cosmetic. However, some plans may partially cover components like extractions, imaging, or crowns. Many practices offer financing through CareCredit or in-house payment plans with 0% interest options.
