What's Typically Included

  • Dental implants (2-4 per arch)
  • Abutments/attachments
  • The snap-on denture prosthesis
  • Some packages also include extractions, temporary dentures during healing, and sedation

Ongoing Costs

  • Attachment replacements: Every 1-2 years due to wear on the snap mechanism
  • Denture base replacement: Every 5-7 years
  • Implant posts themselves: Can last 20+ years or a lifetime with proper care

How It Compares to Other Denture Types

Type Per Arch
Traditional removable (no implants) $1,000 - $3,000
Snap-on (implant-retained) $8,000 - $17,000
Fixed implant dentures (All-on-4, etc.) $18,000 - $40,000+

The main cost advantage of snap-on over fixed is that fewer implants are needed (2-4 vs. 4-8), and the surgical procedure is less complex. The tradeoff is less stability and the need to replace attachments periodically.

Key Factors That Affect Price

  • Number of implants: More implants provide greater stability but increase cost. A 2-implant overdenture is the most affordable entry point, while 4+ implants offer superior function and force distribution.
  • Material quality: Premium materials like zirconia implants and high-density acrylic teeth offer enhanced aesthetics and durability but cost more.
  • Preparatory treatments: Bone grafting or tooth extractions are often essential but may not be included in the base price.
  • Location: Metro areas like DC/Northern Virginia tend to run 20-30% higher than smaller cities.

Insurance Coverage

  • Many dental insurance plans provide partial coverage: basic plans may cover 25-50% of the denture portion but rarely cover implants, while comprehensive plans might cover 30-50% of both implants and dentures.
  • Most insurance plans pay out between $1,500 and $2,000 per year toward dental work.
  • Medicare typically does not cover dentures or implants.
  • Many practices offer financing through CareCredit or in-house payment plans.
